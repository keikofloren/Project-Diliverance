Goer

Keiko Ang

Despite having been to Indonesia countless times to visit my extended family, I was still taken aback by the harsh living conditions of Timor Leste. Electricity blackouts were common, hot water was a luxury, and the abundance of cockcroaches kept me constantly on my toes due to my terrible fear of insects.

However, what really stood out to me and challenged my faith was the resilience of the Timorese in serving God and His people, and to worship Him joyfully amidst these circumstances. If I had been put in the same circumstances as them - facing persistent, violent persecution for my faith - would I still have been able to worship God with that same passion? And how can I learn to be more grateful for what God has blessed me with back in Singapore, to be more willing to serve Him joyfully with these gifts?
